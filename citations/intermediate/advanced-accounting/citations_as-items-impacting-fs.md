# Citations — Intermediate P1 Ch 7: Accounting Standards Based on Items Impacting Financial Statements

Every concept cited in the notes and question bank for this chapter (AS 4,
AS 5, AS 11, AS 22), with the primary-source basis a reviewer should
spot-check. Positions are stated in original words, with deliberate
near-verbatim exceptions (marked ※ below) where exam answers require the
definitional/operative wording: the AS 4 definition of events after the
balance sheet date ("those significant events, both favourable and
unfavourable, that occur between the balance sheet date and the date on
which the financial statements are approved"), the AS 5 definitions block
(ordinary activities; extraordinary items "clearly distinct from the
ordinary activities of the enterprise and, therefore, are not expected to
recur frequently or regularly"; prior period items "errors or omissions in
the preparation of the financial statements of one or more prior periods"),
the AS 5 policy-change grounds, the AS 11 monetary-item definition and
closing-rate rule, the AS 11 forward-contract premium/discount and
remaining-maturity marking wording, and the AS 22 prudence pair
("reasonable certainty" / "virtual certainty supported by convincing
evidence") with the enacted-or-substantively-enacted measurement rule.
Everything else: original wording vs SM Ch 7 Units 1–4.

## Numerical conventions used (not statutory citations)

- MCQ arithmetic verified by `scripts/verify_numerical/verify_as-items-impacting-fs.py`
  (22/22 pass, this session).
- cs-001 (Godavari, AS 4): insolvency provision 4,50,000 × 80% = 3,60,000;
  suit top-up 3,10,000 − 2,50,000 = 60,000 — both adjusting events; the fire
  and the proposed dividend change nothing at the year end.
- cs-002 (Narmada, AS 11): initial recording 40,000 × 83.00 = 33,20,000;
  creditor restated at closing 84.00 → loss 40,000 to P&L; forward premium
  (84.20 − 83.60) × 40,000 = 24,000 over 3 months → 8,000 expensed by 31 Mar —
  the SM's forward-contract illustration uses the same two formulas.
- cs-003 (Tungabhadra, AS 22): taxable income 10,00,000 + 50,000 + 80,000 −
  1,30,000 = 10,00,000 → current tax 3,00,000; DTL 39,000 − DTA 24,000 = net
  deferred charge 15,000; total 3,15,000 = 30% × (book profit + permanent
  difference) — the reconciliation check the SM illustrations use.
- cs-004 (Bhima, AS 5): prior-period wages 1,80,000 through the current P&L;
  revised-life depreciation (9,00,000 − 4,00,000) ÷ 2 = 2,50,000.
