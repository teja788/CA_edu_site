# Citations — Intermediate P1 Ch 8: Revenue Based Accounting Standards

Every concept cited in the notes for this chapter (AS 7 Construction
Contracts, AS 9 Revenue Recognition), with the primary-source basis a
reviewer should spot-check. Positions are stated in original words, with
deliberate near-verbatim exceptions (marked ※ below) where exam answers
require the definitional/operative wording: the AS 7 construction-contract
definitional tail ("closely interrelated or interdependent in terms of their
design, technology and function or their ultimate purpose or use"), the
cost-plus definition ("allowable or otherwise defined costs, plus a
percentage of these costs or a fixed fee"), the AS 9 revenue definition
("gross inflow of cash, receivables or other consideration arising in the
course of the ordinary activities"), and the sale-of-goods recognition heads
(property/significant risks and rewards transferred with no effective
control retained; no significant uncertainty as to consideration).

| Ref | Position relied on | Used in |
|---|---|---|
| SM Ch 8 U1 (AS 7) ※ | Scope: accounting in the financial statements of CONTRACTORS (customer = contractee); two-party requirement (own engineering division building for own production = self-constructed asset under AS 10); construction-contract definition — specifically negotiated for an asset or a combination of assets closely interrelated or interdependent in terms of their design, technology and function or their ultimate purpose or use (near-verbatim); includes directly related services (project managers, architects) and destruction/restoration contracts | notes §1 |
| SM Ch 8 U1 (AS 7) | Segmenting one contract (all three: separate proposals; separate negotiation with accept/reject choice per asset; identifiable costs and revenues per asset) vs combining several contracts (all three: single package; in effect one project with an overall profit margin; concurrent or continuous sequence); additional customer-ordered asset = separate contract if it differs significantly in design/technology/function OR its price is negotiated without regard to the original price | notes §1 |
| SM Ch 8 U1 (AS 7) ※ | Fixed price contract (fixed sum or fixed rate per unit of output, cost-escalation clauses do not change the category) vs cost-plus contract (reimbursement of allowable or otherwise defined costs, plus a percentage of these costs or a fixed fee — near-verbatim) | notes §1; WE 1 |
| SM Ch 8 U1 (AS 7) | Contract revenue composition: initial agreed amount + variations, claims and incentive payments only to the extent PROBABLE of resulting in revenue AND capable of reliable measurement; escalations added, penalties for late completion deducted | notes §1; WE 1 |
| SM Ch 8 U1 (AS 7) | Contract costs: direct (site labour incl. supervision, materials, depreciation and hire of contract plant, moving plant/materials, contract-specific design, estimated rectification/guarantee incl. expected warranty costs, third-party claims — reduced by incidental income such as surplus-material or end-of-contract plant sales); allocable (insurance, non-specific design, construction overheads on NORMAL activity levels, AS 16 borrowing costs); costs specifically chargeable to the customer; excluded (general administration and R&D without specified reimbursement, selling costs, idle-plant depreciation) | notes §1 |
| SM Ch 8 U1 (AS 7) | Percentage of completion once the outcome is reliably estimable — fixed price needs all FOUR: total revenue reliably measurable; inflow probable; BOTH costs to complete AND stage at reporting date reliably measurable; contract costs clearly identifiable and reliably measurable so actuals compare with prior estimates. Cost-plus needs TWO: inflow probable; costs clearly identifiable and reliably measurable whether or not specifically reimbursable | notes §1 |
| SM Ch 8 U1 (AS 7) | Outcome not reliably estimable: revenue only to the extent of costs incurred whose recovery is probable, contract costs expensed as incurred (zero profit, not necessarily zero revenue); switch to percentage of completion when the uncertainty lifts | notes §1 |
| SM Ch 8 U1 (AS 7) | Expected loss: when total contract costs are expected to exceed total contract revenue, the ENTIRE expected loss is recognised as an expense immediately, irrespective of stage of completion (prudence) | notes §1; WE 2 |
| SM Ch 8 U1 (AS 7) | Stage measures: cost-proportion (costs for work performed to date / estimated total costs), surveys of work performed, physical proportion; progress payments and advances received are NOT stage measures; future-activity costs (materials delivered but unused) excluded from costs-to-date and carried as contract WIP if recovery probable; recognised revenue later doubtful of collection = expense, never a revenue adjustment | notes §1; WE 2 |
| SM Ch 8 U1 (AS 7) | Cumulative application to current estimates; revision of revenue/cost/outcome = change in accounting estimate under AS 5 — current and future periods, no restatement | notes §1 |
| SM Ch 8 U1 (AS 7) | Disclosures: period contract revenue, methods for revenue and for stage; contracts in progress: aggregate costs incurred + recognised profits (less recognised losses), advances received, retentions; definitions of retentions / progress billings / advances; net position per contract = costs + recognised profits − recognised losses − progress billings → gross amount due FROM customers (asset) or TO customers (liability) | notes §1 |
| SM Ch 8 U2 (AS 9) ※ | Revenue definition: gross inflow of cash, receivables or other consideration arising in the course of the ordinary activities (near-verbatim) from sale of goods, rendering of services, and use by others of enterprise resources yielding interest, royalties and dividends; excluded special-consideration streams: construction contracts (AS 7), hire-purchase and lease agreements, government grants (AS 12), insurance-contract revenue of insurers | notes §2 |
| SM Ch 8 U2 (AS 9) | Ordinary-activities sorting (real-estate dealer's plot = full revenue; manufacturer's used machine = disposal gain/loss only); items outside the definition (disposal/holding gains on non-current assets, unrealised current-asset holding gains, natural increases in herds/forest products, forex gains, obligation-discharge and obligation-restatement gains) | notes §2 |
| SM Ch 8 U2 (AS 9) | Agency: revenue is the COMMISSION, not the gross inflow; principal vs agent by who bears the risks and rewards (must-provide-itself vs merely arranges); indirect taxes collected (GST) are nobody's revenue — a liability payable to government | notes §2 |
| SM Ch 8 U2 (AS 9) ※ | Sale-of-goods recognition: (1) property in the goods transferred for a price, OR all significant risks and rewards of ownership transferred with no effective control retained to a degree usually associated with ownership; AND (2) no significant uncertainty as to the amount of consideration (near-verbatim heads); proviso — recognise only if at the time of performance it is not unreasonable to expect ultimate collection; where property and risk diverge, revenue follows the risks and rewards (seller-favour transit insurance example) | notes §2 |
| SM Ch 8 U2 (AS 9) | Timing scenarios: bill and hold (delivery delayed at buyer's request) — sale complete when goods are specifically identified, delay is at the buyer's request, and goods are ready for delivery; sale on approval (formal acceptance / act adopting / rejection-period lapse); installation (complex-and-significant → wait for completion, simple → on agreement to purchase; separate installation fee = service revenue on completion); sale-and-repurchase = financing (borrowing; repurchase excess = interest); consignment (revenue only on onward sale; unsold stock = consignor's inventory); trade discounts and volume rebates deducted from revenue vs cash discounts expensed | notes §2; WE 3 |
| SM Ch 8 U2 (AS 9) | Rendering of services: proportionate completion method (performance of more than one act) vs completed service contract method (single act, or unperformed acts so significant that performance is incomplete — revenue on the sole/final act); same collection proviso | notes §2 |
| SM Ch 8 U2 (AS 9) | Interest / royalties / dividends: recognise only when no significant uncertainty as to measurability or collectability; interest on a time proportion basis (outstanding × rate); royalties on an accrual basis per the terms of the agreement; dividends when the owner's right to receive payment is established (declaration date, not proposal or holding period) | notes §2 |
| SM Ch 8 U2 (AS 9) | Collection uncertainty: doubtful AT the time of raising the claim (escalations, export incentives, penal interest with poor recovery) → postpone to the extent of uncertainty and recognise when reasonably certain (later receipt is NOT a prior period item, and per AS 5 not extraordinary); uncertainty arising AFTER recognition → separate provision / bad-debt write-off, never adjust revenue; instalment terms alone never delay recognition | notes §2 |
| Revised ICAI scheme (Aug 2024, via Ch 3) | Applicability paragraph: AS 7 and AS 9 bind every entity in full — neither appears on any SMC or MSME relief list | notes intro |

## Numerical conventions used (not statutory citations)

- No numerical verify script exists yet (`scripts/verify_numerical/` has no
  revenue module — the chapter has no question bank); the three worked
  examples were re-computed by hand during the 2026-07 content audit:
- WE 1 (Dhavala, AS 7): revenue 800 + 15 (25% × 60 material escalation) +
  30 (labour increase passed in full — the 20% wage rise is within the 30%
  ceiling) + 40 incentive + 75 variation + 15 claim = ₹9,75,00,000. The stem
  was reworded in the audit so the 30% reads unambiguously as a ceiling on
  the wage-rise percentage, not a 30%-of-increase pass-through.
- WE 2 (Kaveri, AS 7): costs-to-date 420 − 20 unused site materials = 400;
  stage 400/1,000 = 40%; revenue 900 × 40% = 360; expected loss 1,000 − 900
  = 100 = 40 already in P&L + 60 provision. The ₹600 lakh cost-to-complete
  is stated to include consuming the ₹20 lakh site materials.
- WE 3 (Sindhu, AS 9): 80,000 (bill and hold) + 1,40,000 (70% of consignment
  sold onward) + 60,000 (approved portion) + 4,80,000 (5,00,000 less 4%
  trade discount) = ₹7,60,000.
