## Schedule II, Companies Act 2013 (See section 123) — Part A, para 1

- **Bare-act line relied on:** "Depreciation is the systematic allocation of
  the depreciable amount of an asset over its useful life. The depreciable
  amount of an asset is the cost of an asset or other amount substituted for
  cost, less its residual value. The useful life of an asset is the period over
  which an asset is expected to be available for use by an entity, or the
  number of production or similar units expected to be obtained from the asset
  by the entity."

## AS 10 (Property, Plant and Equipment) — change of method / revision of estimates

- **Position relied on (paraphrase, not quoted):** a change in the method of
  depreciation is treated as a change in accounting ESTIMATE and applied
  prospectively; residual value and useful life are reviewed at least each
  year-end, and a revision is applied prospectively — the carrying amount
  (less revised residual) is spread over the remaining revised life. Confirmed
  present in the current ICAI SM Ch 5 (May 2026 onwards edition) in the same
  terms, 3 Jul 2026.
- **Used in:** q-f1c5-011, q-f1c5-012, q-f1c5-013, q-f1c5-033, q-f1c5-034,

## AS 10 — commencement of depreciation (ready for use)

- **Position relied on (paraphrase):** depreciation begins when the asset is
  available for use (in the location and condition necessary to operate),
  not when actual production starts.

## AS 26 (Intangible Assets) — amortisation of finite-life intangibles

- **Position relied on (paraphrase):** intangible assets with finite useful
  lives are amortised over those lives.
