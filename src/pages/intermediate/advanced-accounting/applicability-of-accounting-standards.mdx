Twenty-seven standards exist, but not everyone carries all of them. A two-partner firm
with a ₹3 crore turnover does not owe the world segment reports and diluted EPS. This
chapter is the sorting machinery: **companies** split into SMCs and non-SMCs under the
AS Rules 2021, and **non-company entities** — under the ICAI's revised scheme effective
1 April 2024 — split into **MSMEs and Large entities**. Know the classification criteria
to the rupee and the exemption lists standard by standard; this chapter's questions are
pure recall with traps at the boundaries.

## 1. Companies: SMC or non-SMC

For companies, the Government's classification has **two levels**: **Small and
Medium-sized Companies (SMCs)** — a term the AS Rules 2021 (notified 23 June 2021)
define — and everyone else (**non-SMCs**). Non-SMCs
comply with **all** standards in their entirety; SMCs get a short list of exemptions and
relaxations.

An **SMC** is a company that clears *all five* gates: its equity or debt securities are
**not listed** (nor in the process of listing) on any stock exchange, in India or
outside; it is **not a bank, financial institution or insurance company**; its
**turnover (excluding other income) did not exceed ₹250 crore** in the immediately
preceding accounting year; its **borrowings (including public deposits) did not exceed
₹50 crore at any time** during the immediately preceding accounting year; and it is
**not a holding or subsidiary company of a non-SMC**. Fail any one gate and the company
is a non-SMC.

**What an SMC may skip:**

- **AS 17 (Segment Reporting)** — not applicable in its entirety.
- **AS 20** — exempt from disclosing **diluted** EPS (including and excluding
  extraordinary items).
- **AS 28** — may measure *value in use* as a **reasonable estimate** rather than by the
  present-value technique (and skip the related discount-rate disclosures).
- **AS 29** — paragraphs 66–67 disclosures not required.
- **AS 19** — specified lease-disclosure paragraphs not required.
- **AS 15** — relaxations for non-vesting short-term compensated absences, discounting,
  and other long-term benefits. For **defined benefit plans**, an SMC whose average
  number of employees is **50 or more** must still determine the liability actuarially
  by the **Projected Unit Credit Method** with a government-bond discount rate; an SMC
  averaging **fewer than 50 employees** may instead estimate the accrued liability by
  **any other rational method**. Only that sub-50 carve-out overlaps the full-blown
  "any rational method" escape that otherwise belongs to non-company MSMEs.
- **AS 25** — interim reporting only bites if a regulator requires it (SEBI's quarterly
  results catch certain non-SMCs) or the company elects it.

Note what is *not* on the list: **AS 3 is not an SMC exemption** — a company's cash flow
statement relief comes only from s.2(40) (OPC, small company, dormant company), a
different gate entirely.

## 2. Non-company entities: MSME or Large

The revised ICAI scheme (accounting periods commencing on or after **1 April 2024**)
classifies non-company entities — firms, LLPs, trusts, societies — into **MSMEs** and
**Large entities**. An **MSME** is a non-company entity that clears *all five* gates:

1. Equity or debt securities **not listed** nor in the process of listing, in India or
   outside;
2. **Not a bank, financial institution or insurance company**;
3. **Turnover (excluding other income) ≤ ₹250 crore** in the immediately preceding
   accounting year;
4. **Borrowings ≤ ₹50 crore at any time** during the immediately preceding accounting
   year;
5. **Not a holding or subsidiary of a non-MSME**.

The conditions are tested **as at the end of the relevant accounting period**. A
**Large entity** is simply any non-company entity that is not an MSME — and it complies
with **every standard in full**. Entities that a regulator routes to Ind AS (or specific
AS) are outside this announcement altogether.

## 3. What MSMEs skip

- **Not applicable at all, to any MSME**: **AS 3** (Cash Flow Statements), **AS 17**
  (Segment Reporting), **AS 20** (EPS), **AS 24** (Discontinuing Operations).
- **AS 18 and AS 28 not applicable in their entirety** to the *smaller* MSMEs — those
  with turnover **≤ ₹50 crore**, borrowings **≤ ₹10 crore** (same test windows), and not
  holding/subsidiary of an MSME outside this sub-class. Larger MSMEs apply AS 18, and
  apply AS 28 with relaxations (reasonable-estimate value in use; fewer disclosures).
- **Relaxations**: encouraged-disclosure paragraphs of **AS 10** (para 87) and **AS 11**
  (para 44); specified **AS 19** lease disclosures; **AS 26** disclosure paras;
  **AS 29** paras 66–67; and **AS 15** — where, unlike SMCs, an MSME may account for
  defined benefit plans and other long-term benefits by **any rational method** (e.g.
  assuming all benefits fall due at the year end) instead of actuarial valuation.
- **AS 22** — an MSME complies for **current tax** only (recognition, measurement,
  presentation per the specified paragraphs); on first availing this, the accumulated
  **deferred tax balance is adjusted against opening revenue reserves**.
- **AS 14 and the JCO/JCA parts of AS 27** — treated as not applicable because such
  transactions rarely occur in MSMEs; if they do occur, the standard applies.
- **AS 21, 23, 27 (CFS aspects), 25** — conditional for every non-company entity: they
  apply only if the entity is required by a regulator, or elects, to prepare
  consolidated statements or interim reports.

## 4. The housekeeping rules

Four administrative rules travel with the MSME exemptions and recur in exams:

1. **Disclosure of status** — an MSME availing exemptions must state in a note that it
   is an MSME and has complied with the standards as applicable to MSMEs.
2. **Ceasing to be an MSME** — the full standards apply **from the current period**;
   comparatives need **not** be restated merely because status changed, but both facts
   (previous MSME status and unrevised comparatives) must be disclosed.
3. **Becoming an MSME** — no exemptions until the entity has remained an MSME for
   **two consecutive years**.
4. **Partial availing** — an MSME may pick and choose which exemptions to use, with
   disclosure of the standards involved, provided the selection does not mislead users.

### Walkthrough — sorting four entities

A partnership with turnover ₹240 crore and peak borrowings ₹45 crore, unlisted → clears
every gate → **MSME**. An LLP with turnover ₹260 crore → fails the ₹250-crore gate →
**Large**, full compliance. A firm of any size that is a subsidiary of a Large entity →
**not an MSME**, however small its own numbers. An unlisted company with turnover ₹180
crore and borrowings ₹30 crore → an **SMC** (the company scheme, not the MSME scheme) —
it skips AS 17 and diluted EPS but still prepares a cash flow statement unless it is an
OPC, small or dormant company under s.2(40).

## 5. The chapter in one paragraph

Companies: SMC (all five gates — unlisted, not bank/FI/insurer, turnover ≤ ₹250 crore,
borrowings ≤ ₹50 crore, no non-SMC group ties) or non-SMC; SMCs skip AS 17 and diluted
EPS, relax AS 15/19/28/29, but keep actuarial PUC for defined benefits (unless the SMC
averages fewer than 50 employees, when another rational method suffices) and keep AS 3.
Non-companies from 1 April 2024: MSME (same five gates, tested at the period end) or
Large; MSMEs drop AS 3/17/20/24 entirely, the ≤₹50-crore/≤₹10-crore sub-class drops
AS 18/28 too, AS 22 shrinks to current tax with deferred balances adjusted to opening
reserves, AS 15 admits any rational method, and AS 14/27-JCO apply only if the
transactions exist. Housekeeping: disclose MSME status; on cessation apply standards
currently without restating comparatives; two consecutive years before regaining
exemptions; partial availing allowed with disclosure. Consolidation and interim
standards bite only those required, or electing, to prepare them.
